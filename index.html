Correlations Discovered Between Age/Smoking

There is a general upward (positive) correlation between age and percent of smokers. It appears that older people are more likely to be smokers.

While we cannot provide conclusive results without further research, one could speculate reasons behind this correlation.

For example, it is possible that smoking percentages are higher among older people because the health risks of smoking were not as widely understood a few decades ago.
